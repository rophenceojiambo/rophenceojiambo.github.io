Hi, I am Rophence!

I pursued a Master of Science in Biostatistics at Brown University School of Public Health in Providence, Rhode Island, United States. I was a Graduate Research Fellow in the Brown Moi Partnership for Biostatistics Training in HIV (NAMBARI), a research training program co-led by Principal Investigators Joseph Hogan, ScD and Ann Mwangi, PhD that aims to build biostatistics capacity for HIV research at Moi University.

Prior to joining Brown, I earned my Bachelor of Science in Applied Statistics with Computing degree at Moi University, Eldoret, Kenya and graduated with a First Class Honors. My research interests revolve around harnessing the power of machine learning algorithms to analyze complex HIV-related datasets, identify patterns, and extract meaningful insights. By employing advanced statistical models and data-driven approaches, I aim to improve our understanding of HIV transmission dynamics, disease progression, and treatment outcomes. My ultimate goal is to contribute to the development of more effective strategies for HIV prevention, diagnosis, and therapeutic interventions through innovative and data-informed approaches in the realm of clinical trials.

During my previous research project, I had the privilege of working with, Jon Steingrimsson, who served as my thesis advisor. Our research endeavor aimed to extend the findings of a cluster randomized control trial for cardiovascular risk reduction. Specifically, I focused on generalizing these findings to a target population derived from electronic health records. By leveraging the baseline covariate information contained within these records, my objective was to explore the applicability and effectiveness of the interventions studied in the trial on a broader scale.
